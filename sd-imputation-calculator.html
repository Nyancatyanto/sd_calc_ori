t-stat / P-value / CI for MD → SD  ·  Cochrane §6.5.2.3
SE = |MD| ÷ t   |   SE = (upper − lower) ÷ divisor
SD = SE × √(NE × NC ÷ (NE + NC))

For the difference between two group means (not a single group's CI). Assumes equal SD in both groups — the result is one SD, entered for both arms. df = NE + NC − 2.

Not in Cochrane Handbook — general statistics
One-sample t‑stat / P‑value → SD
t = mean ÷ SE   →   SE = |mean| ÷ t
SD = SE × √n

Use this only when a paper reports a one-sample t-test result for a single group — for example, testing whether the mean change from baseline differs from zero within one arm (t or P from that test, plus the mean and n). This is the standard one-sample t-statistic relationship, not a method from the Cochrane Handbook's imputation sections — if you cite this in a manuscript, cite a general statistics reference, not Cochrane §6.5. Degrees of freedom = n − 1.
